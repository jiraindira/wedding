Around the Area

St Emillion

As some of you may know, we got engaged in Saint – Emilion. This place is very dear to our hearts. Moreover, it has a lot to offer to wine lovers, foodies and architecture appreciators. Endless vineyards, the fresh amazing air and silence during sunrise, cute little streets and cozy restaurants where you are treated as a royalty and where people take pride of their locally sourced ingredients. There are numerous wine tours one can take to learn more about wine making process, meet the owners, buy a few bottles. Our favorites are Chateau Fonplegade (where he asked the question and she said yes) and Chateau Gaudet (where the caves are located completely underground, the entire tour is mesmerizing). But don’t worry, there are hundreds of others that, we are confident, are equally interesting and beautiful.

We are also excited to let you know about Annual Heritage Night that will be held in Saint Emilion on Saturday, September 17th 2016, just a couple days before our wedding. On this day, Saint-Emilion is celebrating its uniqueness as UNESCO landscape. The highlight of the day and weekend besides other activities is the fantastic light show and unforgettable fireworks. Just something to think about when planning your visit to Bordeaux.

Other than St Emillion, here are some other notable places that you might be interested in:

Archachon

Arcachon is a seaside town in southwest France that's known for oyster harvesting. According to our oyster connoisseur, Natalia Plekhanova, we had some of the finest oysters at L'Oyster Bar.

South of the town is Dune du Pilat, Europe’s longest and highest dune of sand, which reaches a mind-boggling 107 meters in height! Naturally, at the top of the dune you’ll stunning views of the entirety of Arcachon Bay!

(45 Min Drive)

Bordeaux

Bordeaux, a World Herritage Site, is a charming city with a serene feel to it. The city center has many 18th-century buildings, a pedestrian-friendly boulevard, and a thriving waterfront area with chic boutiques and cafés. There’s also world-class art, as well as theater and top dining options.

(30 Min Drive)

Supermarket

There is a supermarket 3 minute drive from the Chateau. You will be able to get most of your essentials from this place.

Alternatively, you could drive 10 minutes to Carrefour, one of the largest hypermarket chains in the world, in Creon. To our pleasant surprise, they have a lot of very good wine at a reasonable price.

Medoc

Medoc is a region of France well known as a wine growing region. The region has around 1,500 vineyards and is the home to some of the most prestigious wine villages (Pauillac, Margaux, St-Julien, and St-Estèphe), whose châteaux—among them Margaux, Mouton, Lafite Rothschild, and Latour—produce some of the world’s most expensive wines.

(120 Min Drive)

Creon

Creon is a small town north-east of Chateau Lagorce. You will find most of your essentials in this town; cafes, cute shops and hair salons!

(10 Min Drive)
