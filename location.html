Chateau Lagorce

Chateau Lagorce

On a hillside overlooking a valley, surrounded by forests and rolling terrain, Château Lagorce has the advantage of being located near the city of Bordeaux and amid the famed vineyards of the region - St Emilion.

The name 'Lagorce' comes from a type of vegetation that appeared in the early 16th century, and according to a document dating to the 1700s, there was originally a moat surrounding the castle. With its oldest tower boasting walls 1.2 meters thick, the château was defensible during the feudal wars of the 15th and 16th centuries, the two seeing destruction of parts of the castle.

The property changed hands and families many times over the centuries, occasionally seeing periods of restoration. Then in the 19th century, the Faux family became successful vignerons planting Barsac and Sauternes vines. Records indicate that their wines sold at twice the price of others in the region because of its high quality. The property was sold in 1859 to Jules Beyssac, whose heirs lived on the estate until 1956. Nowadays the lovely castle is owned by the Holmes family that lives on premises and serves as coordinators and organizers of the events held at the place.

The location of Château Lagorce is ideal. It is only minutes from both the Garonne and Dordogne rivers, a half hour from twenty excellent golf courses, and surrounded by the vineyards of the Bordeaux and St-Emilion region. The ocean is not too far away, and guests can spend an enjoyable day in Arcachon reached by car in about 45 minutes. Dining out at fine restaurants will be an adventure in just deciding where to go!

The castle itself is surrounded by 70 acres of vineyards, caves and walks, with a lake just to the south of the Chateau. There is also an enclosed 25mx7m pool, which gives piece of mind with any children that may be coming to the wedding. The grand distinction of the castle is monumental steps that are reputed to have been built for Napoleon Bonaparte’s visit of the village of Haux a few centuries ago.




Getting there

By Air

Bordeaux-Mérignac Airport is a 35-minute drive away. We discovered that the cheapest way to get to Bordeaux would be to take a flight to any big European city (see Milan, Paris, Barcelona, London, etc.) and then take a train (Euro Star - is the popular one that has a number of rides from anywhere in Europe) or a flight from a budget airline like EasyJet or RyanAir. We would also suggest to search for cheaper flight on the websites like SkyScanner and everyone’s favorite Kayak

Also, the Chateau is 1 hour 30 minutes from Bergerac Airport if you decide to fly there.

By Train

Bordeaux Saint-Jean Train Station is a 20 minute drive away. SNCF and RailEurope trains leave several times daily and connect with Bordeaux via Paris (departing Gare Monparnasse) with a total journey time of approximately 4h. If you search for tickets in advance, there is a chance to score good price

Whether you are taking a flight or train, you will most likely need to hire a car to get from the airport or train station to the Chateau. Please visit the following sites to get the rates and make a booking:

  • Priceline
  • Europe Car
  • Auto Europe
  • Sixt

Alternatively, catch a local train from St Jean train station to Portets station - a 5 minute drive from the Chateau. We can organize for someone to pick you up! If you decide to do this , please let us know in advance.

Taxi fares to the Chateau will cost approximately €70-€100 each way

By Car

If you are renting a car, please visit the following sites to get the rates and make a booking:

  • Priceline
  • Europe Car
  • Auto Europe
  • Sixt

Most of the big-time US rental agencies have offices throughout Europe, as do the two major Europe-based agencies, Europe Car and Sixt. Shop around! We have rented car twice in Bordeaux for about $50 a day (before cost of full insurance).

Also, almost all car rentals are manual transmission. We highly advise you to get a car with GPS and full insurance.
